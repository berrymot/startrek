Novels

Too many timelines

The shows and movies are, for the most part, set in the Prime timeline. Exceptions are the Mirror universe episodes of TOS, DS9, ENT, DIS; and the Kelvin-timeline movies.

The shows tend to ignore the novels entirely, i.e. not mention them or their events, BUT we have no evidence they don't happen in the prime timeline.

But because of Star Trek: Picard (and less so Prodigy), which both have their own associated novels, some of the novels have to be shoved into a branch of the novel timeline so there are no clashes. This is achieved with the Coda trilogy, which retroactively puts all the novels between First Contact and the 2387 Romulan Supernova into what is referred to in-universe as the First Splinter. Which is stated as branching out from P.

There are also four Kelvin-timeline novels.
